Stream-of-Thought Note Taking

Stream-of-Thought (SoT) note-taking aims to model ideas that can later be crafted and polished. The goal is just to get the raw ingredients on paper.

It biases towards fast, concise, deliberate written bullet points. It prevents run-on long-winded sentences, limits double-takes and does not allow deletion.

I decided to turn this into a game. Here are the rules:

Enabled controls:

  1. Everything must be a bullet point

  2. Bullet points must be 100 characters or less

  3. Enter: Start a new thought or idea (i.e. a bullet point)

  4. Indent (push): Provide additional details related to the previous idea

  5. Unindent (pop): Start a new idea unrelated to the previous point

Disabled controls:

  1. Up: You cannot go back and edit a previous bullet point

  2. Backspace/delete: You cannot edit or delete anything that was written down

  3. Thinking a thought thatâ€™s not written down

  4. Over-thinking before writing something down

  5. Grammar or spell-check

At first glance, this might seem like making a bullet point list. The difference lies in the mindset shift when you understand who the target audience is.
