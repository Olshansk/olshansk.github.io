Finance & Investing - Living Legends

The individuals below are the ones who defined invented and defined what all the different forms of investing we’re familiar with today: value, quantitative, activist, macroeconomic, speculative, etc… Without them, we wouldn’t have the same understanding or instruments which are taken for granted.

Warren Buffett (1930–present) | Berkshire Hathaway

Who they are: Value investor and business magnate. Chairman and CEO of Berkshire Hathaway.

Impact: Buffet re-defined value investing. He initially focused on undervalued companies following Ben Graham’s “cigar butt” approach. He shifted his focus to high-quality businesses with lasting competitive advantages, emphasizing long-term investments and moats in fundamentally sound companies.

Best Investment: Buffett’s investment in Coca-Cola is considered to be one of his cornerstone investments. His $1.3B investment in 1988 is worth ~$25B in 2024 earning ~$736M in annual dividends.

Best resource: I’ve been reading his Annual Shareholder Letters (1977–2023) for almost a decade now and don’t plan on stopping anytime soon.

George Soros (1930–present) | Soros Fund Management

Who they are: Hedge fund manager primarily known for macroeconomic speculation.

Impact: Soros is famous for "breaking the Bank of England" in 1992 with a $1 billion profit from shorting the British pound. He also developed the theory of reflexivity in markets.

  • Best resource: The Alchemy of Finance by George Soros

Ray Dalio (1949–present) | Bridgewater Associates

  • Who they are: Founder of Bridgewater Associates, the world's largest hedge fund.

  • Impact: Dalio pioneered macroeconomic investing and created the "Principles" framework for decision-making, which has been influential beyond finance.

  • Best resource: Principles by Ray Dalio

Stanley Druckenmiller (1953–present) | Duquesne Capital

  • Who they are: Hedge fund manager and macroeconomic trader.

  • Impact: Druckenmiller is known for his high-risk, high-reward macro trades, including working with George Soros during the British pound short. His focus on flexibility and market timing set him apart.

  • Best resource: The New Market Wizards (Interview with Druckenmiller)

Ed Thorp (1932–present) | Princeton/Newport Partners

  • Who they are: Mathematician, hedge fund manager, and quantitative investing pioneer.

  • Impact: Thorp used mathematical models to develop groundbreaking quantitative strategies in finance. He’s also famous for inventing card-counting in blackjack.

  • Best resource: A Man for All Markets by Ed Thorp

Peter Lynch (1944–present) | Fidelity Magellan Fund

  • Who they are: Legendary mutual fund manager who ran the Fidelity Magellan Fund.

  • Impact: Lynch made the Magellan Fund one of the best-performing mutual funds ever. His "buy what you know" philosophy empowered retail investors to take charge of their portfolios.

  • Best resource: One Up on Wall Street by Peter Lynch

Carl Icahn (1936–present) | Icahn Enterprises

  • Who they are: Corporate raider and activist investor.

  • Impact: Icahn pioneered activist investing, using large stakes in companies to influence corporate governance, often leading to restructuring or management changes.

  • Best resource: King Icahn: The Biography of a Renegade Capitalist by Mark Stevens

Michael Burry (1971–present) | Scion Asset Management

  • Who they are: Hedge fund manager and contrarian investor.

  • Impact: Burry became famous for predicting and profiting from the 2008 subprime mortgage crisis. His contrarian approach, looking for mispriced assets, has gained widespread recognition.

  • Best resource: The Big Short by Michael Lewis

Paul Tudor Jones (1954–present) | Tudor Investment Corporation

  • Who they are: Hedge fund manager with a focus on macroeconomic and commodities trading.

  • Impact: Jones is famous for anticipating and profiting from the 1987 stock market crash, earning him a reputation as one of the best traders in history.

  • Best resource: Paul Tudor Jones: The Art of Aggressive Trading (Interview in Market Wizards)

Bill Gross (1944–present) | PIMCO

  • Who they are: Bond investor and co-founder of PIMCO.

  • Impact: Known as the "Bond King," Gross built PIMCO into a bond investing giant and shaped modern fixed-income portfolio management.

  • Best resource: Everything You've Heard About Investing Is Wrong by Bill Gross

Finance & Investing - Special Shoutouts

Charlie Munger (1924–2023) | Berkshire Hathaway

Who they were: Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway. Longtime partner of Warren Buffett.

Impact: Munger is a master of mental models and long-term value investing. His multidisciplinary approach to investing has been highly influential.

  • Best resource: Poor Charlie’s Almanack by Charlie Munger

Jim Simons (1938–2024) | Renaissance Technologies

Who they are: Mathematician and founder of Renaissance Technologies. The most successful quant hedge fund.

  • Impact: Simons pioneered quantitative trading, using mathematical models and algorithms to dominate markets. His Medallion Fund is one of the best-performing funds in history.

  • Best resource: The Man Who Solved the Market by Gregory Zuckerman
