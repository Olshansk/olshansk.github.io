Whenever someone sends me a “long” (more than 5 minute voice note), they usually end with an apology. My usual response is: “Don’t apologize.”

No one ever asks for an apology when scheduling a 30 minute call without an agenda. No one apologizes if a meeting goes 5 minutes over. No one apologizes for sending a hello without context (see nohello.net). No one apologizes for sending text messages without having fully thought through why they need your attention.

My opinion on this is clear and simple: We need to normalize long voice notes as a tool in our communication toolbox.

I’ve found that the tools social media platforms give us end up driving how we communicate. The basic ones are obviously text, video and audio chats, but it gets more nuanced.
