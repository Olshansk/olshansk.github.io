The message I sent to Stephan:

Wanted to share a thought regarding the fake deadlines post that you called out.

Firstly, I completely agree with everything you said.

This post from a couple of years ago has been one of the most impactful in terms of a real behavioral change that I’ve made in leading my team on a day-to-day basis. Highly recommend if you haven’t already read it.

https://staysaasy.com/startups/2023/11/10/imprecise-asks.html

TL;DR tell people how long you want them to spend on something so they don’t go into the deep end, and time-box appropriately.

Now, for myself, in order to balance, fake deadlines, but also not give ourselves too much time, I’ve created ship ranges.

The goal is to ship between date X and Y, which means that X is a north star and Y is the absolute last date. It’s been working pretty well, as long as I push everyone to try and meet X as hard as possible without burning anyone out.

It also works for dinner parties where ask people to arrive, for example, between 630 and 7:00 which implies not after 7:00 and not before 6:30.
