## CV / Personal Statement

My educational and professional experience has been very multidisciplinary, and what I've found is that it always comes down to making tradeoffs and aligning incentives: technical, operational, and the most complex ones involving human judgment and experience.

My undergraduate thesis at the University of Toronto balanced algorithmic optimization, distributed computing, and GPU acceleration in fMRI analysis. Google and Twitter sharpened my instincts around technical and product tradeoffs in understanding what users actually want. Magic Leap taught me how to align incentives in a cross-functional hardware-first environment where research, engineering, marketing, product, and financing were all pulling in different directions. At Waymo, I focused on capturing quality in labeling pipelines to build reliable ground truth and evals, where rigorous evaluation design was everything. Crypto brought protocol design that leverages economic incentives in adversarial networks, with distributed systems and containerized infrastructure at the core. And in construction, the challenge is working with people in the industry to help them understand what AI technology can and cannot do.
