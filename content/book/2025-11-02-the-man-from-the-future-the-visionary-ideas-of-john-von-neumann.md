tl;dr John Von Neuman is a legend, but this book did not do him justice. I highly recommend reading the first two chapters, but there's no reason to keep going after that.

## Review of the Book's Writing

The book starts of strong, engaging and full of interesting stories of how John Von Neuman grew up. It was an insight into how he became the man he was.

But, after the first couple of chapters, it just turned into a historical account of the Manhattan Project, and the role he played. If it had been from his perspective, or had drawn on his own challenges, I'd love it. Unofrunatly, it just felt like a historical play by play.

I realized I was forcing myself to keep listening for the sake of finishing the book. After two chapters of doing so, I gave up.

I really wish he would have had the opportunity to write his own autobiography, so hopefully an alternative universe has that pleasure.

## Overall Perspective of John Von Neuman

John is the result of Nature, Nurture and luck. He was a prodigy, born at the right time, in the right place, in the right environment and into the right family.

He was gifted with a mathematical foundation, and had the opportunity to cultivate his curiosity.
