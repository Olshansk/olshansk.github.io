Today, the three reasons I work out are:

1. I want to be able to eat more food
2. I'm uncomfortable at the gym, and I feel uncomfortable when I start feeling comfortable
3. I get all my best ideas when I'm working out

If I'm being really honest though, there are like tons more:

- Feel comfortable in my own body (when I look in the mirror)
- Need a place to listen to podcasts (at 0.8x speed)
- Need a place to disconnect (headphones in, glasses off)
- Practice new skills (to get the mind in different state)
- Look good (to feel good) but also because it's key to first impressions
- Make me feel more confident in my own skin

In the past, the three reasons I worked out were:

1. Push myself to my limits (until a hit a series of injuries)
2. Get as fit as possible (until I realized I only care to be in the top 0.01% professionally)
3. Impress other people (both men and women); but that only matters in your early 20s

My three laws of training are:

1. Find a set of activities you enjoy doing; _and fit into your lifestyle_
2. Don't get injured; _especially one-way injuries_
3. Stay consistent by making adjustments when you stop enjoying it and not getting injured

I have lot more to say on this but figured I'd share my immediate thoughts in the meantime.
