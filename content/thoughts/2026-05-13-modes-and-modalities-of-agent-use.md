Like everyone else, I've been exploring different ways to use AI day-to-day.

As someone who doesn't naturally multitask, but knows when it's necessary, I've fallen into the trap of always having at least half a dozen agents working for me in parallel.

There is a time and place to do so, but I think it's important to realize that, as Greg Brockman said, the bottlneck will be [human attention](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bBS93A0BeNI).

This is just a quick set of notes to capture what I've been thinking about recently, and I hope to expand on it in detail in a future post.

# Modalities of Agent Use

- **CLI (Terminal)**: Codex, Claude and Gemini CLI all have their strengths and weaknesses.
- **Application (Desktop)**: Claude for work
- **Chat**: Whatsapp, classic chat
- **Audio Out (Podcast)**: Notebook LLM
- **Audio In**: Whisper, voice, etc
- **Audio I/O (Conversation)**: Claude or ChatGPT, whi
- **Vision In**: Remarkable Table.
- **Vision Out**: HTML
