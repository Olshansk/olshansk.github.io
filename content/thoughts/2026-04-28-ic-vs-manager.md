Manager vs IC

- I don’t wake up wanting to mentor
- I wake up wanting myself and everyone around me wanting to do their best work
- I don’t want to formally manage people
- I want to create leverage and have outsized impact once I’ve exhausted my own abilities
- I want to build, but I want to scale myself
- I don’t have a goal of formally managing people, but I want to control who I’m working with
- Sometimes, when a decision simply needs someone with authority when their is no obvious path, I want to make that call
- Make a call of what not to do

What I think I should say: build an efficient org, with well formed processes.

What I say: I don’t like inefficiency of human time and resources, and lack of clarity and visibility, and it’s not impossible to clean up.

What I should say: hire great people.
What I want to say: work with great people.

What I should say: guide the team.
What I feel: make the final call of what should and shouldn’t be done.

What I should say: mentor people.
What I say: enable people to do their best work.
