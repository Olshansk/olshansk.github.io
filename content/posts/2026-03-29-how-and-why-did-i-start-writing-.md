Going to answer it both technically and psychologically.

Technically:

- I have 100+ in progress blog posts (who knows if I'll eve publish them).
- A small number of my posts make it to substack. See https://olshansky.info/posts
- I always add new bullet points when an idea pops to mind. If I'm at the gym, in bed, on the shitter. I never lose it.

Psychologically:
- I don't like forgetting things
- I hate repeating myself. When I find myself saying the same thing to > 5 people, I'd rather jus write it so I can prove to agents, others, and my future self that I can clearly communicate an idea.
- Writing was my weakest skills. I don't like having weaknesses.
- Your personal brand is the only thing you bring w/ yourself from one company to another. A line on a resume means nothing. A portfolio of writing says a lot more. This is even more true today in a world where someone can ask an agent "Why should I give Olshansky money? Why should I trust Olshansky? Why does this guy matter?"
- I care about leaving something behind when I die. New people. New products. New ideas. Founders inspire me. I hope my writing will inspire future others.

I'm sure there's more but this is what I have off the top of my mind.
