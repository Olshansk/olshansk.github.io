WIPA - Leadership notes (blog post)

This is for engineering leaders managing a team of 2-8

  • know when to do, lead, architect, micro manage, research, etc.. it’s never just one and will change for each individual all the time

  • As much as engineers hate meetings, don’t skip more than a couple 1:1s, especially if someone is in a whole

  • If someone is really struggling and you can tell they’re anxious. Give them a few days. If it lasts a few weeks, assure them they’re not getting fired if that’s the case and that you only want to help

  • Don’t just create recurring meetings (syncs, checking, retros, etc…) forever. Creating quarters of high meetings and quarters of low meetings. Keep it fresh

  • Never have everyone working on tech debt but there are times for everyone to be working on new features

  • There’s a time and place to micromanage

  • Don’t make all meetings silent meetings but make some of them

  • If you don’t have time to implement features yourself, and you’re not in the nitty gritty day to day, you have to jump in and , fix bugs, and implement features every once in a while. It’s your responsibility to make sure everything stays cohesive.

  • It’s your responsibility to read the docs they write

  • It’s your responsibility to see what the real dev ex is like, not just because a tool is cool and fancy

  • It’s your responsibility to go into checkin meetings prepared to guarantee that they’re effective

  • Make technical questions and answers documented - it’s hard but worth it

  • Talk through “why something is a certain way”, document “how” it works

  • When someone leaves, have them document not just “how”, but “why” because that part is impossible to figure it out.

  • Tell the engineers when they have liberty and freedom and when they have to follow strict guidelines

  • Once in a while I give a good presentation, speech or thought leadership. They want me to do that all the time, but the reason I can is because I go back to doing research and work

  • Talk about my three off the cuff inspirations

  • Reasure high-perfomers they’re not getting fired

  • On meetings, there is no “right amount”

    • It’s not just 1 weekly meeting in perpetuity

    • It’s not just 5 meetings in perpetuity

    • You need space/room to be adhoc

    • You need structure

    • You need change to make sure things don’t get scale and boring

    • Meeting decisions need to come from the top

    • It’s an art more than a science

  • Looking young and not having a family definitely does get in the way

  • Your energy will not always persist. You need someone else there when it doesn’t

  • When you delegate, you also have to be okay with the quality of work decreasing - ideally it won’t, but it can

  • If you’re motivated and have a vision, you’re looking for people to help you get there. If you’re demotivated or lost the vision, you need a very strong partner to help you.

  • Whenever one of your reports is presenting something, don’t just be an active listener. Take notes w/ feedback (both good, room for improvement and bad, and share it right after)

  • WIP leadership note -> environment where you slow someone down

    • Not afraid to ask stupid questions as the leader

    • Place where everyone can ask everything

    • Leader

  • For long comments or link dumping, ask everyone to provide a tl;dr

    • Screenshots with boxes an arrows

    • Long comments with a summary

    • Links with a motivation

    • You ask for it and everyone appreciates it

      • Feels a bit micro managy but it bears fruit

  • Ask questions on behalf of the team

  • Be able to zoom in and zoom out

  • Always give people a few different things to work on

    • Review

    • Do

    • Research

    • No one should ever be blocked

  • Need to be responsive

  • Need to congratulate achievements but not congratulate everything
